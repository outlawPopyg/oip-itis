<p><b>System testing</b>, a.k.a. <b>end-to-end (E2E) testing</b>, is testing conducted on a complete <a href="/wiki/Software_system" title="Software system">software system</a>.
</p><p>System testing describes testing at the system level to contrast to testing at the <a href="/wiki/System_integration_testing" title="System integration testing">system integration</a>, <a href="/wiki/Integration_testing" title="Integration testing">integration</a> or <a href="/wiki/Unit_testing" title="Unit testing">unit</a> level.
</p><p>System testing often serves the purpose of evaluating the system's compliance with its specified <a href="/wiki/Requirements" class="mw-redirect" title="Requirements">requirements</a> <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> – often from a <a href="/wiki/Functional_requirements" class="mw-redirect" title="Functional requirements">functional requirement specification</a> (FRS), a <a href="/wiki/Requirements_analysis" title="Requirements analysis">system requirement specification</a> (SRS), another type of specification or multiple. 
</p><p>System testing can detect defects in the system as a whole.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>System testing can verify the design, the behavior and even the believed expectations of the customer. It is also intended to test up to and beyond the bounds of specified software and hardware requirements.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Destructive_testing" title="Destructive testing">Destructive testing</a>: tests are carried out to the specimen's failure, in order to understand a specimen's performance or material behaviour under different loads.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nondestructive_testing" title="Nondestructive testing">Nondestructive testing</a>: analysis techniques to evaluate the properties of a material, component or system without causing damage.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fault_injection" title="Fault injection">Fault injection</a>: A testing technique which stress the system in an unusual way to examine the system behavior.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li></ul>
