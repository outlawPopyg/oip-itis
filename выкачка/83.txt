<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-right" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Continuous_Integration.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Continuous_Integration.jpg/220px-Continuous_Integration.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="116" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Continuous_Integration.jpg/330px-Continuous_Integration.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Continuous_Integration.jpg/440px-Continuous_Integration.jpg 2x" data-file-width="834" data-file-height="440" /></a><figcaption>Sketch of <a href="/wiki/Flow_diagram" title="Flow diagram">flow diagram</a> for continuous integration</figcaption></figure>
<p><b>Continuous integration</b> (<b>CI</b>) is the practice of integrating <a href="/wiki/Source_code" title="Source code">source code</a> changes frequently and ensuring that the integrated codebase is in a workable state.
</p><p>Typically, developers <a href="/wiki/Merge_(version_control)" title="Merge (version control)">merge</a> changes to an <a href="/wiki/Branching_(revision_control)" class="mw-redirect" title="Branching (revision control)">integration branch</a>, and an automated system <a href="/wiki/Software_build" title="Software build">builds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Software_testing" title="Software testing">tests</a> the <a href="/wiki/Software_system" title="Software system">software system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-martinfowler_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-martinfowler-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
Often, the automated process runs on each <a href="/wiki/Commit_(version_control)" title="Commit (version control)">commit</a> or runs on a schedule such as once a day. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Grady_Booch" title="Grady Booch">Grady Booch</a> first proposed the term CI in <a href="/wiki/Booch_method" class="mw-redirect" title="Booch method">1991</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> although he did not advocate integrating multiple times a day, but later, CI came to include that aspect.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1251242444" /><table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="14" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></span></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.  <span class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">August 2014</span>)</i></span></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The earliest known work (1989) on continuous integration was the Infuse environment developed by G. E. Kaiser, D. E. Perry, and W. M. Schell.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 1994, Grady Booch used the phrase continuous integration in <i>Object-Oriented Analysis and Design with Applications</i> (2nd edition)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> to explain how, when developing using micro processes, "internal releases represent a sort of continuous integration of the system, and exist to force closure of the micro process".
</p><p>In 1997, <a href="/wiki/Kent_Beck" title="Kent Beck">Kent Beck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Jeffries" title="Ron Jeffries">Ron Jeffries</a> invented <a href="/wiki/Extreme_programming" title="Extreme programming">extreme programming</a> (XP) while on the <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Comprehensive_Compensation_System" title="Chrysler Comprehensive Compensation System">Chrysler Comprehensive Compensation System</a> project, including continuous integration.<sup id="cite_ref-martinfowler_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-martinfowler-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="This reference citation appears to be to a self-published source. (May 2020)">self-published source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Beck published about continuous integration in 1998, emphasising the importance of face-to-face communication over technological support.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In 1999, Beck elaborated more in his first full book on Extreme Programming.<sup id="cite_ref-Beck,_Extreme_Programming_Explained_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beck,_Extreme_Programming_Explained-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/CruiseControl" title="CruiseControl">CruiseControl</a>, one of the first open-source CI tools,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Self-published_sources" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="This reference citation appears to be to a self-published source. (May 2020)">self-published source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> was released in 2001.
</p><p>In 2010, Timothy Fitz published an article detailing how <a href="/wiki/IMVU" title="IMVU">IMVU</a>'s engineering team had built and been using the first practical CD system. While his post was originally met with skepticism, it quickly caught on and found widespread adoption<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Lean_software_development" title="Lean software development">lean software development</a> methodology, also based on IMVU.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Practices">Practices</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practices"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The core activities of CI are developers co-locate code changes in a shared, integration area frequently and that the resulting integrated codebase is verified for correctness. The first part generally involves merging changes to a common version control branch. The second part generally involves automated processes including: building, testing and many other processes.
</p><p>Typically, a <a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">server</a> builds from the integration area frequently; i.e. after each commit or periodically like once a day. The server may perform <a href="/wiki/Quality_control" title="Quality control">quality control</a> checks such as running unit tests<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and collect <a href="/wiki/Software_quality" title="Software quality">software quality</a> metrics via processes such as static analysis and performance testing.
</p>

</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Build_automation">Build automation</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Build automation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Build_automation" title="Build automation">Build automation</a> is a best practice.<sup id="cite_ref-Brauneis,_&#91;OSLC&#93;_Possible_new_Working_Group_-_Automation_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brauneis,_[OSLC]_Possible_new_Working_Group_-_Automation-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Taylor,_Rails_Deployment_and_Automation_with_ShadowPuppet_and_Capistrano_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Taylor,_Rails_Deployment_and_Automation_with_ShadowPuppet_and_Capistrano-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Atomic_commits">Atomic commits</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Atomic commits"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>CI requires the version control system to support <a href="/wiki/Atomic_commit" title="Atomic commit">atomic commits</a>; i.e., all of a developer's changes are handled as a single commit.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Committing_changes">Committing changes</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Committing changes"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>When making a code change, a <a href="/wiki/Software_developer" class="mw-redirect" title="Software developer">developer</a> creates a branch that is a copy of the current <a href="/wiki/Codebase" title="Codebase">codebase</a>. As other changes are committed to the <a href="/wiki/Source_code_repository" class="mw-redirect" title="Source code repository">repository</a>, this copy diverges from the latest version.
</p><p>The longer development continues on a branch without merging to the integration branch, the greater the risk of multiple integration conflicts<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and failures when the developer branch is eventually merged back. When developers submit code to the repository they must first update their code to reflect the changes in the repository since they took their copy. The more changes the repository contains, the more work developers must do before submitting their own changes.
</p><p>Eventually, the repository may become so different from the developers' baselines that they enter what is sometimes referred to as "merge hell", or "integration hell",<sup id="cite_ref-Cunningham,_Integration_Hell_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cunningham,_Integration_Hell-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> where the time it takes to integrate exceeds the time it took to make their original changes.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Testing_locally">Testing locally</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Testing locally"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Proponents of CI suggest that developers should 
use <a href="/wiki/Test-driven_development" title="Test-driven development">test-driven development</a> and to
ensure that all <a href="/wiki/Unit_testing" title="Unit testing">unit tests</a> pass locally before committing to the integration branch so that one developer's work does not break another developer's copy. 
</p><p>Incomplete features can be disabled before committing, using <a href="/wiki/Feature_toggle" title="Feature toggle">feature toggles</a>.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Continuous_delivery_and_continuous_deployment">Continuous delivery and continuous deployment</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Continuous delivery and continuous deployment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Continuous_delivery" title="Continuous delivery">Continuous delivery</a> ensures the software checked in on an integration branch is always in a state that can be deployed to users, and <a href="/wiki/Continuous_deployment" title="Continuous deployment">continuous deployment</a> automates the deployment process.
</p><p><i>Continuous delivery</i> and <i>continuous deployment</i> are often performed in conjunction with CI and together form a CI/CD pipeline. 
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Version_control">Version control</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Version control"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Version_control" title="Version control">Version control</a></div>
<p>Proponents of CI recommend storing all files and information needed for building in <a href="/wiki/Version_control" title="Version control">version control</a>, (for <a href="/wiki/Git" title="Git">git</a> a <i>repository</i>); that the system should be buildable from a fresh checkout and not require additional dependencies. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Martin_Fowler_(software_engineer)" title="Martin Fowler (software engineer)">Martin Fowler</a> recommends that all developers commit to the same integration branch.<sup id="cite_ref-Fowler,_Continuous_Integration_practices_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fowler,_Continuous_Integration_practices-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> 
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Automate_the_build">Automate the build</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Automate the build"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Build_automation" title="Build automation">Build automation</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/List_of_build_automation_software" title="List of build automation software">Build automation tools</a> automate building. 
</p><p>Proponents of CI recommend that a single command should have the capability of building the system. 
</p><p>Automation often includes automating the integration, which often includes <a href="/wiki/Software_deployment" title="Software deployment">deployment</a> into a production-like <a href="/wiki/Deployment_environment" title="Deployment environment">environment</a>. In many cases, the build script not only compiles binaries but also generates documentation, website pages, statistics and distribution media (such as Debian <a href="/wiki/Deb_(file_format)" title="Deb (file format)">DEB</a>, Red Hat <a href="/wiki/RPM_Package_Manager" title="RPM Package Manager">RPM</a> or Windows <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Installer" class="mw-redirect" title="Microsoft Installer">MSI</a> files).
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Commit_frequently">Commit frequently</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Commit frequently"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Developers can reduce the effort of resolving conflicting changes by synchronizing changes with each other frequently; at least daily. Checking in a week's worth of work risks conflict both in likelihood of occurrence and complexity to resolve. Relatively small conflicts are significantly easier to resolve than larger ones. Integrating (committing) changes at least once a day is considered good practice, and more often better.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Daily_build">Daily build</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Daily build"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daily_build" title="Daily build">Building daily</a>, if not more often, is generally recommended.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Every_commit_should_be_built">Every commit should be built</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Every commit should be built"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The system should build commits to the current working version to verify that they integrate correctly. A common practice is to use Automated Continuous Integration, although this may be done manually. Automated Continuous Integration employs a continuous integration server or <a href="/wiki/Daemon_(computer_software)" class="mw-redirect" title="Daemon (computer software)">daemon</a> to monitor the <a href="/wiki/Revision_control" class="mw-redirect" title="Revision control">revision control system</a> for changes, then automatically run the build process.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Every_bug-fix_commit_should_come_with_a_test_case">Every bug-fix commit should come with a test case</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Every bug-fix commit should come with a test case"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>When fixing a bug, it is a good practice to push a test case that reproduces the bug. This avoids the fix to be reverted, and the bug to reappear, which is known as a <a href="/wiki/Regression_testing" title="Regression testing">regression</a>.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Keep_the_build_fast">Keep the build fast</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Keep the build fast"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The build needs to complete rapidly so that if there is a problem with integration, it is quickly identified.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Test_in_a_clone_of_the_production_environment">Test in a clone of the production environment</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Test in a clone of the production environment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1236090951" /><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Test_environment" class="mw-redirect" title="Test environment">Test environment</a></div>
<p>Having a <a href="/wiki/Test_environment" class="mw-redirect" title="Test environment">test environment</a> can lead to failures in tested systems when they deploy in the <a href="/wiki/Production_environment" class="mw-redirect" title="Production environment">production environment</a> because the production environment may differ from the test environment in a significant way. However, building a replica of a production environment is cost-prohibitive. Instead, the test environment or a separate <a href="/wiki/Pre-production_environment" class="mw-redirect" title="Pre-production environment">pre-production environment</a> ("staging") should be built to be a scalable version of the production environment to alleviate costs while maintaining <a href="/wiki/Technology_stack" class="mw-redirect" title="Technology stack">technology stack</a> composition and nuances. Within these test environments, <a href="/wiki/Service_virtualisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Service virtualisation">service virtualisation</a> is commonly used to obtain on-demand access to dependencies (e.g., APIs, third-party applications, services, <a href="/wiki/Mainframe_computer" title="Mainframe computer">mainframes</a>, etc.) that are beyond the team's control, still evolving, or too complex to configure in a virtual test lab.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Make_it_easy_to_get_the_latest_deliverables">Make it easy to get the latest deliverables</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Make it easy to get the latest deliverables"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Making builds readily available to stakeholders and testers can reduce the amount of rework necessary when rebuilding a feature that doesn't meet requirements. Additionally, early testing reduces the chances that defects survive until deployment. Finding errors earlier can reduce the amount of work necessary to resolve them.
</p><p>All programmers should start the day by updating the project from the repository. That way, they will all stay up to date.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Everyone_can_see_the_results_of_the_latest_build">Everyone can see the results of the latest build</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Everyone can see the results of the latest build"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>It should be easy to find out whether the build breaks and, if so, who made the relevant change and what that change was.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Automate_deployment">Automate deployment</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Automate deployment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Most CI systems allow the running of scripts after a build finishes. In most situations, it is possible to write a script to deploy the application to a live test server that everyone can look at. A further advance in this way of thinking is <a href="/wiki/Continuous_deployment" title="Continuous deployment">continuous deployment</a>, which calls for the software to be deployed directly into production, often with additional automation to prevent defects or regressions.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Benefits">Benefits</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Benefits"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1251242444" /><table class="box-More_citations_needed_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div class="mbox-image-div"><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></span></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a href="/wiki/Special:EditPage/Continuous_integration" title="Special:EditPage/Continuous integration">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>&#32;in this section. Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span>  <span class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">May 2016</span>)</i></span><span class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<small><a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this message</a></small>)</i></span></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>CI benefits include:
</p>
<ul><li>Facilitates detecting <a href="/wiki/Software_bug" title="Software bug">bugs</a> earlier</li>
<li>Reduces effort to find cause of bugs; if a CI test fails then changes since last good build contain causing change; if build after each change then exactly one change is the cause<sup id="cite_ref-martinfowler_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-martinfowler-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Avoids the chaos of integrating many changes</li>
<li>When a test fails or a bug is found, reverting the codebase to a good state results in fewer lost changes</li>
<li>Frequent availability of a known-good build for testing, demo, and release</li>
<li>Frequent code commit encourages modular, less complex code<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Quick feedback on system-wide impact of code changes</li>
<li>Supports collection of <a href="/wiki/Software_metrics" class="mw-redirect" title="Software metrics">software metrics</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Code_coverage" title="Code coverage">code coverage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Code_complexity" class="mw-redirect" title="Code complexity">code complexity</a></li></ul>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Risks">Risks</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Risks"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Risks of CI include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Build_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Build system">Build system</a> setup requires effort<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Writing and maintaining an automated test suite requires effort</li>
<li>Value added depends on the quality of tests<sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>High build latency (sitting in queue) limits value<sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Implies that incomplete code should not be integrated which is counter to some developer's preferred practice<sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Safety and mission-critical development assurance (e.g., <a href="/wiki/DO-178C" title="DO-178C">DO-178C</a>, <a href="/wiki/ISO_26262" title="ISO 26262">ISO 26262</a>) require documentation and review which may be difficult to achieve</li></ul>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Best_practices_for_cloud_systems">Best practices for cloud systems</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Continuous_integration&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Best practices for cloud systems"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The following practices can enhance productivity of <a href="/wiki/CI/CD" title="CI/CD">pipelines</a>, especially in systems hosted in the <a href="/wiki/Cloud_computing" title="Cloud computing">cloud</a>: <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Number of Pipelines</b>: Small teams can be more productive by having one <a href="/wiki/Repository_(version_control)" title="Repository (version control)">repository</a> and one pipeline. In contrast, larger organizations may have separate repositories and pipelines for each team or even separate repositories and pipelines for each service within a team.</li>
<li><b>Permissions</b>: In the context of <a href="/wiki/CI/CD" title="CI/CD">pipeline-related</a> permissions, adhering to the <a href="/wiki/Principle_of_least_privilege" title="Principle of least privilege">principle of least privilege</a> can be challenging due to the dynamic nature of <a href="/wiki/Software_architecture" title="Software architecture">architecture</a>. Administrators may opt for more permissive permissions while implementing compensating <a href="/wiki/Security_controls" title="Security controls">security controls</a> to minimize the blast radius.</li></ul>
